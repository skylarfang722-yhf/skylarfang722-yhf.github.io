Overview

In this stock picking analysis, I applied a multi-dimensional assessment system using the Finviz screener to find fundamentally reliable stocks with reasonable valuation. Based on key ideas from financial statement analysis, I selected representative indicators across price, profitability, financial leverage, and liquidity to build an integrated screening framework. After screening, I filtered 13 stocks across different industries, suggesting the selected ratio combination does not bias results toward a single sector.

Framework

Screening logic: choose companies that are reasonably priced, profitable, operationally efficient, financially stable, and liquid enough for short-term obligations.

  • Valuation: P/E < 15, P/S < 1
  • Profitability: ROE > 15%, Operating Margin > 10%
  • Risk control: Debt/Equity < 1
  • Liquidity: Current Ratio > 2

These six ratios form a balanced and robust screening method. Together, they target companies with reasonable valuation, strong profitability, high efficiency, stable financing, and good short-term financial condition.

Results

  • Screened 13 stocks across multiple industries (finance, energy, technology, etc.).
  • Industry coverage suggests the framework avoids excessive concentration in one category.
  • The final output is a shortlist suitable for deeper company-level research.

Case Study 1 — Value Stock: Walmart (Summary)

I used Walmart as a value-stock example because it reflects stability and long-run competitive advantages rather than rapid expansion. Quantitatively, Walmart shows stable profitability and manageable leverage. Qualitatively, it benefits from scale-based cost advantages, diversified products, and a loyal customer base, which support stable performance through economic cycles.

Case Study 2 — Growth Stock: NVIDIA (Summary)

I used NVIDIA as a growth-stock example because it matches key growth-investing characteristics: strong future growth potential, high reinvestment, competitive advantages, and broad market prospects.

Quantitative highlights:

  • Strong profit growth and high profitability (high ROE and profit margin).
  • Higher P/E is normal for growth stocks when investors price in future earnings expansion.

Qualitative highlights:

  • Moat: leading GPU technology + CUDA ecosystem creates high switching costs and long-term competitive edge.
  • Large market potential: positioned in fast-growing sectors such as AI, data centers, cloud computing, and autonomous driving.
  • Innovation structure: heavy R&D supports continuous product improvement and expansion into new opportunities.
  • Operational strength: very high profit margins suggest both growth and efficiency.
